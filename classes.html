Yoga offers physical and mental health benefits for people of all ages and, regardless of your level of yoga expertise, if you're practicing regularly, you can feel better from head to toe. Nadi Yoga Studio offers classes that challenge and restore the mind through physical movement and meditation sessions. Ours classes are suitable for all levels of ability and all ages and we encourage each and every Yogi to listen to their bodies and take their time on this enjoyable and fulfilling journey.

Warm Vinyasa Yoga

Vinyasa Yoga mixes movement synchronized with breath in a vigorous style based on a rapid flow through sun salutations, yoga asana and pranayama for increased strength and flexibility. Hot Yoga

By stretching the body, this series releases accumulated lactic acid & scar tissue to heal the body from the inside out. Suitable for all levels.

Ashtanga Yoga

Ashtanga Yoga is a system of yoga that was brought to the modern world by Sri K. Pattabhi Jois. The flow is composed by six series of asana sequences, increasing in difficulty as you move from the primary series on. The fast-paced movements will help to develop your range of motion, balance and strength. Suitable for all levels.

Yin Yoga

Rooted in Chinese principles, Yin Yoga is a passive yoga practice that stretches and releases the connective tissue around the joints. With the use of props, poses are held for 3 to 5 minutes to create space for stillness in a warmed room, so the body and mind have a chance to relax. If you have been struggling with flexibility, this may be the missing link in your practice. Suitable for all levels.

Kids Yoga

Kids/Youth Yoga is a fun & creative class designed to stimulate children's growing curiosity. Playful yoga poses, breathing exercises and relaxation techniques teach children self-expression, positive thinking & environmental awareness. Suitable for children ages 4-10.

Guided Meditation

Mindfulness meditation changes your relationship to painful emotions and stressful events and develops a sense of compassion for yourself and others. Through mindfulness practice and contemplation, it's possible to relax your physical and mental patterns of rigidity, resistance and contraction, and to enter into a state of greater openheartedness and peace. Under the guidance of a instructor, the meditation sessions include guided meditations, poems or inspirational readings, discussions and sometimes, gentle movement or visualizations.
